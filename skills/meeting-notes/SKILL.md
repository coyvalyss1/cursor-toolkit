# Meeting Notes Template

## Purpose
Generate structured meeting notes for business meetings, customer conversations, partnership discussions, and mentorship sessions.

## When to Use
- User asks to create meeting notes or prep
- User mentions an upcoming meeting and wants to prepare
- User wants to document a conversation structure
- User references "meeting format" or "like my last meeting"

## Customization Guidelines

### For Customer/Partnership Meetings:
- Focus sections on: Product feedback, Use cases, Business model, Go-to-market, Partnership opportunities
- Include demo/walkthrough section
- Add pricing and ROI discussion
- Include follow-up and next steps section

### For Mentorship/Advisory Meetings:
- Focus sections on: Their experience, Lessons learned, Current challenges, Strategic advice
- Include "Honest Critique" section
- Add tactical/specific questions relevant to their expertise
- Include book recommendations or resources section at top if they've shared any

### For Investor Meetings:
- Focus sections on: Traction metrics, Business model, Market opportunity, Team, Funding ask
- Include "Investment Thesis" section
- Add "Concerns & Objections" section
- Include follow-up process and timeline

## Writing Voice

**Meeting notes style:**
- Bullet points only; no paragraphs
- Questions are direct and conversational
- Group related questions under clear subtopics
- Use bold for action items: **[Share demo]**, **[Send metrics]**
- Keep purpose statement concise (1-2 sentences max)
- "What I'm Looking For" = clear expectations, not vague hopes

**Structure principles:**
- Start broad (context/background) → narrow to specific questions
- Early sections = their experience/expertise
- Middle sections = specific to your situation
- End with advice, next steps, staying connected
- Always include Notes, Action Items, Follow-up sections
- Always include Pre-Meeting Preparation section

## Output Format

1. Generate the full meeting notes as a markdown file
2. Save to `[BUSINESS_PLANNING_FOLDER]/Meeting - [Person Name] - [Date].md`
3. Brief summary of what was created and key sections included
